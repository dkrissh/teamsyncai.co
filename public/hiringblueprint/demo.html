How it works

Three steps.
One Hiring Blueprint.

Most hiring tools give you a template and wish you luck. HiringBlueprint builds an interview blueprint around your specific role — then keeps working after the hire.

1
Before the interview
We calibrate the role with AI.

We start by asking you the questions most hiring managers have never been asked. What does success look like at 90 days — specifically? What kind of person always fails in this role? How much autonomy does this role actually require in the first 30 days?

Your answers train the AI on your exact hire. Not on a job title. Not on an industry average. On the specific reality of your team and this role.

The AI asks between 5 and 10 probing questions depending on role complexity — a straightforward role takes fewer, a management or technical role goes deeper.

2
During the interview
AI builds your complete interview system.

From your calibration answers, the AI generates weighted evaluation goals, behavioural interview questions mapped to outcomes, and follow-up probes for when answers get vague.

Every candidate gets evaluated the same way. Not based on who had a better Tuesday. Not on gut feel. On criteria you defined before the first resume came in.

3
After the hire
AI tells you if you were right.

Most hiring tools stop at the offer letter. HiringBlueprint sends you check-in questions at 30, 60, and 90 days — tied to the exact success indicators you defined at the start.

Over time, the AI learns what good hiring looks like for your team specifically. Every hire you make improves the next one.
